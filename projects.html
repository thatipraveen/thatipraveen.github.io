AWS Skill Builder — Enterprise Admin Platform

A flagship platform enabling enterprises to manage training, licensing, and learner progress at global scale.

Context

AWS Skill Builder serves millions of learners worldwide, but enterprise customers lacked a unified way to manage training assignments, licenses, and progress across large organizations.

Existing workflows were fragmented, manual, and not designed for enterprise scale—creating operational overhead for customers and limiting adoption within large accounts.

The Problem

The platform needed a first-party enterprise administration experience that could:

• Scale to millions of learners
• Support diverse enterprise workflows and org structures
• Integrate cleanly with existing consumer learning systems
• Meet strict security, privacy, and compliance requirements
• Serve as a foundation for future learning products

This was not a feature request—it required rethinking platform boundaries, ownership, and long-term extensibility.

My Role

I led the initiative end-to-end, owning organizational design, technical direction, roadmap execution, and cross-functional alignment.

My responsibilities included:

• Building and leading a 30+ person cross-functional org
• Partnering with Product to define enterprise requirements and roadmap
• Driving architectural decisions for scale and extensibility
• Ensuring operational readiness and long-term maintainability

Key Challenges

Enterprise complexity: Customers had diverse organizational models, approval workflows, and licensing needs that could not be solved with one-size-fits-all designs.

Platform boundaries: The solution needed to coexist with existing consumer learning systems without creating tight coupling or duplicated logic.

Scale & reliability: Admin workflows had to operate reliably at global scale with strong consistency guarantees and auditability.

Key Decisions

We deliberately designed the admin experience as a platform capability, not a standalone UI.

Key decisions included:

• Creating extensible abstractions for assignments, licensing, and org management
• Separating admin concerns from learner-facing experiences
• Designing APIs and data models to support future products
• Prioritizing operability, auditability, and observability

These choices reduced short-term velocity but enabled long-term scalability and reuse.

Outcome & Impact

Enabled enterprise administration for 10M+ learners globally, unlocking adoption within large organizations.

Reduced administrative friction by ~40% through streamlined workflows and automation.

Established a durable platform foundation reused across multiple learning initiatives.

Scaled the engineering organization while maintaining service reliability and operational excellence.

What This Demonstrates

This project reflects my approach to leadership: solving ambiguous problems by aligning people, architecture, and execution around long-term business outcomes—not just shipping features.
