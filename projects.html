AWS Skill Builder — Enterprise Admin Platform

A flagship platform enabling enterprises to manage training, licensing, and learner progress at global scale.

Context

AWS Skill Builder serves millions of learners worldwide, but enterprise customers lacked a unified way to manage training assignments, licenses, and progress across large organizations.

Existing workflows were fragmented, manual, and not designed for enterprise scale—creating operational overhead for customers and limiting adoption within large accounts.

The Problem

The platform needed a first-party enterprise administration experience that could:

• Scale to millions of learners
• Support diverse enterprise workflows and org structures
• Integrate cleanly with existing consumer learning systems
• Meet strict security, privacy, and compliance requirements
• Serve as a foundation for future learning products

This was not a feature request—it required rethinking platform boundaries, ownership, and long-term extensibility.

My Role

I led the initiative end-to-end, owning organizational design, technical direction, roadmap execution, and cross-functional alignment.

My responsibilities included:

• Building and leading a 30+ person cross-functional org
• Partnering with Product to define enterprise requirements and roadmap
• Driving architectural decisions for scale and extensibility
• Ensuring operational readiness and long-term maintainability

Key Challenges

Enterprise complexity: Customers had diverse organizational models, approval workflows, and licensing needs that could not be solved with one-size-fits-all designs.

Platform boundaries: The solution needed to coexist with existing consumer learning systems without creating tight coupling or duplicated logic.

Scale & reliability: Admin workflows had to operate reliably at global scale with strong consistency guarantees and auditability.

Key Decisions

We deliberately designed the admin experience as a platform capability, not a standalone UI.

Key decisions included:

• Creating extensible abstractions for assignments, licensing, and org management
• Separating admin concerns from learner-facing experiences
• Designing APIs and data models to support future products
• Prioritizing operability, auditability, and observability

These choices reduced short-term velocity but enabled long-term scalability and reuse.

Outcome & Impact

Enabled enterprise administration for 10M+ learners globally, unlocking adoption within large organizations.

Reduced administrative friction by ~40% through streamlined workflows and automation.

Established a durable platform foundation reused across multiple learning initiatives.

Scaled the engineering organization while maintaining service reliability and operational excellence.

What This Demonstrates

This project reflects my approach to leadership: solving ambiguous problems by aligning people, architecture, and execution around long-term business outcomes—not just shipping features.

Privacy Governance Platform — 700M+ Devices

A large-scale privacy governance platform enabling auditability, policy enforcement, and regulatory compliance across Amazon’s global device ecosystem.

Context

Amazon’s device ecosystem spans hundreds of millions of active devices globally, each subject to evolving regional privacy regulations such as GDPR, CCPA, and COPPA.

As product velocity increased and regulatory scrutiny intensified, existing privacy controls were fragmented across teams and systems, making it difficult to reason about compliance, audit data usage, or enforce consistent policies.

The Problem

The organization needed a centralized, scalable privacy governance platform that could:

• Enforce privacy policies consistently across services and devices
• Provide strong auditability for regulatory reviews
• Adapt to evolving global regulations without repeated rewrites
• Operate safely at the scale of 700M+ devices
• Enable teams to move fast without violating privacy guarantees

This required aligning legal, compliance, product, and engineering stakeholders around a shared technical foundation.

My Role

I led engineering efforts to design and deliver core components of the privacy governance platform, partnering closely with legal, compliance, security, and senior leadership.

My responsibilities included:

• Defining privacy-first architectural patterns and abstractions
• Driving alignment across multiple engineering teams and orgs
• Ensuring systems met strict auditability and compliance needs
• Balancing regulatory requirements with developer usability

Key Challenges

Regulatory ambiguity: Privacy regulations evolved over time and varied by region, requiring flexible designs rather than hard-coded rules.

Scale: Decisions had to work reliably across hundreds of millions of devices with minimal latency and failure tolerance.

Developer adoption: Privacy controls needed to be easy to use; otherwise, teams would bypass them under delivery pressure.

Key Decisions

We designed the solution around privacy as a platform, not a compliance checklist.

Key decisions included:

• Centralizing policy definition while decentralizing enforcement
• Designing extensible privacy abstractions adaptable to new laws
• Building strong audit trails and data lineage into the system
• Prioritizing safe defaults and guardrails over manual processes

These decisions allowed teams to innovate while maintaining a consistent trust posture.

Outcome & Impact

Enabled privacy governance across 700M+ active devices globally.

Achieved 100% compliance with GDPR, CCPA, and COPPA requirements across covered services.

Improved auditability and reduced regulatory risk through consistent, traceable policy enforcement.

Established a reusable privacy foundation adopted by multiple product teams.

What This Demonstrates

This project demonstrates my ability to operate at the intersection of engineering, regulation, and trust—designing systems that scale globally while enabling teams to move fast within clear guardrails.
